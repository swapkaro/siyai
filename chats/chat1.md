## Assistant

Think of the palette as a **stage with lighting**, not a paint set. Most of the screen is bare stage. Color enters as light.

## The 70 / 20 / 8 / 2 rule

| % of screen | Color | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **70–80%** | Ivory `#FFFDF7` | The canvas. Don't fight it — let it breathe. |
| **15–20%** | Deep Ink `#0D0F17` | Almost entirely as *text*, not as a fill. |
| **5–8%** | Mint `#9AE19D` + Emerald `#40E0B0` | The single primary action. One CTA per view. |
| **2–5%** | Seafoam / Sky | *Atmosphere only.* Halos, washes, gradients — never solid fills. |
| **<1%** | Warm Gold `#F6D58A` | Magic dust. Only when a story is born. |

If you find yourself using more color than this, you've turned light into paint. Pull back.

## Each color's *one job*

- **Ivory** — the background of literally everything. The room.
- **Deep Ink** — text and the rare dark surface (full-screen film viewer, the deep-night moment).
- **Mint → Emerald** — the *single* "do this" button on a screen. Mint at rest, Emerald on hover. Never two mint buttons competing.
- **Seafoam + Sky** — atmospheric pairs. Use them *together*, soft-blurred, at 15–30% opacity. They are the aurora behind the orb, the wash on a hero, the glow under a card. **Never** flat-fill them.
- **Warm Gold** — the *moment of creation*. The "your story is ready" glow. A 1-pixel hairline on a featured card. The italic accent on the final CTA. Use it three times in a product, max.

## How they pair

These are the legal combinations:

- **Ivory + Ink** — the default. Body text, UI, everything. Quiet.
- **Ivory + Mint glow** — primary action moment. The CTA *and* its halo.
- **Ivory + Seafoam→Sky gradient** — emotional hero washes, empty states, ambient strips.
- **Mint + Sky** — only *together* in an aurora halo (radial blur, never a hard stop).
- **Ink + Gold** — the magic moment on a dark surface (CTA section, completion screen).
- **Ink + Mint** — primary button text on dark.

Combinations to avoid:
- Mint *next to* Seafoam *next to* Sky as solid blocks — it becomes a candy stripe.
- Gold as a fill larger than a small badge.
- Two saturated colors meeting at a hard edge (no `linear-gradient(green→blue)` rectangles).
- Mint and Gold competing in the same eye-line.

## A real screen, broken down

Take the marketing hero you're looking at:

- **96%** of pixels are ivory.
- The **headline** is Deep Ink, with one italic word in a *Mint → Sky gradient text-clip* — that's the entire color story above the fold.
- The **orb** (top-right) is a radial halo of Mint→Seafoam→Sky, blurred behind ivory. It doesn't have a hard edge; it's a presence.
- One **Mint CTA button** with an Emerald glow halo.
- That's it. No gold above the fold — it appears later in the dark CTA section, italicizing "make today?".

## The mental model

> **Ivory is the room. Ink is the writing on the wall. Mint is the lamp that's on. Seafoam, Sky and Gold are the light spilling from it.**

If a color isn't *doing* one of those four jobs, it shouldn't be on screen.
