SIYAI · a letter, from us posted, Tuesday morning
Crafted in India winter 2026

Dear stranger,

You probably came here because someone you love is having a birthday, or an anniversary, or just a Tuesday that you’d like to remember. You came here looking for a gift.

We can make you one. A film, one or two minutes long, of a memory you’d like to keep. Your father telling the story of his first car. Your grandmother in her kitchen. Your sister, on the morning of her wedding, before anyone had cried yet.

You tell us about it. We will handle the rest.

We won’t use words like prompt, or generate, or output. We don’t think those words belong in this room. There are people here, all across India, who care a great deal about your story turning out well. They’ll work quietly. We’ll send it to you on WhatsApp, the way good news travels in your family already.

If you decide, later, that you’d also like to keep a voice safe before the chance is gone (a grandmother’s, a father’s, your own), we can hold that too. But there’s no rush. Start with the gift.

The whole thing costs about as much as a nice dinner, once. No subscriptions. No badges. No countdown timers. If something feels off, you can write back, and a person here will reply.

§ about us, in three honest paragraphs

We started SiyAI because we kept noticing that the people we loved were slowly fading from the room. A grandfather who used to tell the same story every Sunday, until one Sunday he didn’t. A mother whose voice used to wake the house, getting quieter. The kind of loss you only see backwards.

And, separately, that gifts had gotten worse. Bouquets. Mugs. A printed photo collage on the second-to-last Sunday before someone’s 60th, because the cousin in Toronto couldn’t fly. None of those said what the family meant to say.

So we built a team. A real one. Editors, sound designers, voice artists, writers, and engineers, working across India. We make AI behave the way the brief asked. The technology is the engine. It’s never the headline.

§ the things we will never do

We’ll never sell your memories to advertisers. We’ll never train other people’s models on your grandmother’s voice. We’ll never bury a delete button. We’ll never put a badge on the page that says “bank-grade encryption” as if that were a feeling.

We will reply if you write. There’s a real inbox at hello@siyai.com. A person on the team usually replies the same day.

§ one practical note on price

A film costs about the price of a nice dinner, once. A voice letter, half that. Group gifts split via UPI. We price in your currency, not by lazy conversion. NRIs in Toronto pay what feels fair in Toronto. NRIs in Patna pay what feels fair in Patna. Nobody gets the worse end of the deal.

If money is the thing in the way, write to us. We have a quiet bursary for families who’d like to make something but can’t. We don’t advertise it. We just have it.

With care,
The folks at SiyAI
P.S.  If you’d rather see something before you write to us, the films we’ve made are at /films. They’re short. We’d be honoured if you watched one.
