FROM THE MAKER · THE NOTE THAT CAME WITH THE FILM

I’ve heard Papa tell the story of leaving Patna probably three hundred times. The bus that broke down outside Mokama. The chai stall run by a man whose name he can’t remember but whose face he can. The road, in the monsoon, in 1973.

For his sixtieth I wanted to give him something to keep. Not a watch. Not a card. The story itself, told the way he tells it, with the road moving the way the road moves in his memory.

I recorded him on a Sunday, two years ago, when I didn’t know I’d ever use it. SiyAI did the rest. The road, the chai stall, the monsoon. He watched it three times on Tuesday morning and didn’t say anything. Then he called me and we both didn’t say anything together.

If you’re thinking about making one of these, the only advice I have is: record the small thing. The chai stall. The man whose name he can’t remember. That’s the bit that holds.
